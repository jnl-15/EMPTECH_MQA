What Is Green House Gases?

Greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions are heat-trapping gases that are mostly produced by burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas), deforestation, and agriculture. These gases include carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide. Climate change is fueled by these emissions, which amplify the natural greenhouse effect and raise global temperatures. The generation of heat and energy, transportation, industry, and changes in land use are the main sectors causing these emissions.

The power industry is the major contributor to global greenhouse gas emissions, mostly by burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas) for electricity and heat generation, accounting for about 40% of energy-related CO2 emissions. These processes emit CO2, CH4, and N2O, which contribute to global warming.

Advanced sensors continuously analyze air composition and automatically adjust filtration intensity depending on pollution levels.
